Who or what is 'Love Bell Green'?

In physical terms, 'Love Bell Green' is a family who are trying to make their area a nicer place to be. But it is also a vision of a place where people can form a community, take pleasure in their surroundings and be proud of where they live.

My name is James and I can often be found wandering the streets with a litter-picker and a blue bag, or taking photos of dumped bags, bits of furniture and overflowing bins on the streets. I often have my two children with me, particularly on the way to and from school. And I'm not usually wandering. I'm just going somewhere. With a litterpicker.

I've been getting hacked off about the way things are since I started having to push a child around in a pushchair. This really opens your eyes. The badly parked cars, the traffic, the crossings, the obstructions... So... you complain. When I had to walk around that car parked over the pavement or across a dropover again, while trying to get my kid to nursery, I started taking photos and reporting the problem. The effect was underwhelming, to say the least.

The council (Lewisham) appear to be a bit rubbish at taking action or communicating. Nothing seems to change.

I got involved with meetings about our pair of Gas Holders, which the council both locally listed the Gas Holders and then practically gave the green light for demolition. I got involved with the meetings which spawned an attempt at gaining Neighbourhood Forum status, which the council rejected. So I decided to do something myself. In another space, I've been photographing construction projects for over 8 years, watching areas get 'regenerated'. I've seen good things and awful things and I can see a vision of Bell Green beyond Warehouse Retail and Car Parks.

The war on litter has been going on since February 2023, when we bought a litterpicker for our son for his birthday. There wasn't a lot of thought about it. It's hard thinking up present ideas! So, in the evening, we went out to try out the litterpicker. We're located halfway along our road. It's not a long road. 60 houses? We only went to the end and filled a whole kitchen liner bag!
