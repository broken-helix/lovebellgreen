Bell Green in an area in south-east London, in the borough of Lewisham. Sandwiched between Forest Hill, Sydenham, Catford and Bellingham, partitioned by a river and railway line and a short walk away from the Borough of Bromley, It is a forgotten land, mostly taken up with a poorly planned retail Park, originally occupied by Sainbury's, before further development.


The site of this retail park was once a gas works, supplying gas for heating and lighting, supplying jobs and industry. So the land is reasonably toxic, though mostly locked away under the car parks and buildings. Any garden areas are sealed off and there are testing pipes dotted all around the area, for regular environmental sampling.
